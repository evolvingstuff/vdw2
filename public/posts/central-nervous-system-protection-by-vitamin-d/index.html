    Vitamin D is a lipid-soluble vitamin that can be found in some foods.     • the role of vitamin D in the pathophysiology of devastating neurological disorders

Vitamin D is a lipid-soluble vitamin that can be found in some foods. It is also produced endogenously (in the presence of ultraviolet light), transported through the blood to the targets organs and this is the reason to consider vitamin D as a hormone. This review is focused mainly on 

* the vitamin D receptors, 

* the importance of vitamin D as a neuromodulator,
