      Vitamin D Receptor rs2228570 Gene Polymorphism Is Associated with Asthma Severity and Exacerbations

      Sekiko Uehara, Keita Hirai , Toshihiro Shirai, Hinako Otaki, Taisuke Akamatsu, Kunihiko Itoh

      Biological and Pharmaceutical Bulletin 2025 Volume 48 Issue 1 Pages 86-92 DOI https://doi.org/10.1248/bpb.b24-00684

      Vitamin D plays a crucial role in immune system function. Several studies have indicated that genetic variations in the vitamin D receptor (VDR) and vitamin D binding protein (VDBP, encoded by GC gene) increase the risk of developing asthma. However, the effect of these variations on the prognosis and clinical outcomes of asthma remains unclear. This study, involving 152 adult patients with asthma, aimed to assess the influence of VDR and GC polymorphisms on asthma severity and its exacerbation. Gene polymorphisms previously associated with asthma risk were analyzed, and VDR mRNA expression levels were evaluated in peripheral blood mononuclear cells.

      The AA genotype of the VDR rs2228570 polymorphism was associated with an

      • elevated risk of severe asthma compared to the AG/GG genotype ( odds ratio, 3.20 ; 95% confidence interval [CI], 1.24–8.28).

      Furthermore, patients with the rs2228570 AA genotype showed an

      • elevated risk of exacerbation during the 1-year follow-up period ( hazard ratio, 4.01 ; 95% CI, 1.75–9.15).

      VDR mRNA expression was significantly reduced in patients with the AA genotype. Furthermore, the mRNA expression levels of GLCCI1, HDAC2, NR3C1, and NFE2L2, which are associated with steroid response, were reduced in patients with the AA genotype.

      Our findings indicate that patients with the AA genotype of VDR rs2228570 are more likely to experience severe asthma and exacerbations. This polymorphism has the potential to reduce vitamin D efficacy by altering VDR function and expression, potentially resulting in increased inflammation and reduced steroid responsiveness in patients with asthma.

      The Role of Vitamin D Receptor-Activating Supplements in Asthma Management: A Comprehensive Analysis

      Summary of Key Findings

      Emerging research highlights the potential of various dietary supplements to modulate asthma pathophysiology through activation of the Vitamin D Receptor (VDR). Among the supplements investigated—Magnesium, Omega-3, Resveratrol, Quercetin, Zinc, Boron, Ginger, Curcumin, Progesterone, Sulforaphane, Butyrate, and Berberine

      —three compounds stand out for their robust evidence in asthma models:  Resveratrol ,  Quercetin , and  Curcumin .

      These agents demonstrate synergistic effects with Vitamin D, reduce airway inflammation, and improve clinical outcomes in preclinical and clinical studies.

      Mechanisms of VDR Activation and Asthma Pathophysiology

      The VDR plays a pivotal role in immune regulation and airway homeostasis. Polymorphisms in the VDR gene are associated with increased asthma susceptibility, highlighting its importance in disease modulation 5 14. Vitamin D itself enhances steroid responsiveness by upregulating glucocorticoid receptors (GR-α) and suppressing pro-inflammatory cytokines like IL-17F and IL-4 7 22. Supplements that potentiate VDR signaling may amplify these benefits, particularly in patients with Vitamin D deficiency or steroid resistance.

      Comparative Analysis of Key Supplements

      1) Resveratrol

      Resveratrol, a polyphenol found in grapes and berries, demonstrates multifaceted anti-asthmatic effects:

      • Anti-Inflammatory Action : Reduces eosinophil infiltration, Th2 cytokines (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13), and TGF-β1-driven epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), a key process in airway remodeling 9 10.

      • Synergy with Vitamin D : Enhances VDR-RXR heterodimerization and potentiates 1,25D binding to VDR, amplifying anti-inflammatory effects 11.

      • Clinical Relevance : Suppresses airway hyperresponsiveness (AHR) and inflammation in murine models, with efficacy comparable to corticosteroids but with fewer side effects 10 12.

      2) Quercetin

      Quercetin, a flavonoid abundant in fruits and vegetables, exhibits rapid bronchodilatory and immunomodulatory effects:

      • Bronchodilation : Inhibits phosphodiesterase-4 (PDE4), increasing cAMP levels and relaxing airway smooth muscle 17 18.

      • Immune Regulation : Shifts Th1/Th2 balance by suppressing GATA-3 (Th2 transcription factor) and enhancing T-bet (Th1 promoter), reducing IgE and eosinophil levels 18 20.

      • Oxidative Stress Mitigation : Activates Nrf2/HO-1 pathways, reducing lipid peroxidation and enhancing antioxidant defenses 19.

      3) Curcumin

      Curcumin, the active compound in turmeric, modulates critical inflammatory pathways:

      • PPARγ Activation : Suppresses NF-κB-driven inflammation and mucus hypersecretion in ovalbumin (OVA)-induced asthma models 4.

      • Airway Remodeling : Attenuates subepithelial fibrosis and collagen deposition by inhibiting TGF-β/Smad signaling 19.

      • Clinical Trials : Reduces exacerbations and improves lung function in combination with standard therapies, though direct VDR interactions require further study 4.

      Supporting Evidence for Other Supplements

      • Magnesium : While serum magnesium levels correlate with asthma severity, its role is primarily as a bronchodilator rather than a direct VDR activator 3.

      • Berberine : Limited evidence in asthma; focuses on metabolic and antimicrobial benefits 21.

      • Zinc, Omega-3, Sulforaphane : Absent from the provided studies in an asthma context, though theoretical immune benefits exist.

      Conclusion: Prioritizing Supplements for Asthma Management

      Based on current evidence:

      1. Resveratrol  emerges as the most promising due to its dual anti-inflammatory and VDR-synergistic effects, particularly in steroid-resistant asthma.

      2. Quercetin  offers acute symptom relief and long-term immune modulation, making it ideal for exacerbation management.

      3. Curcumin  provides robust anti-remodeling effects, though further research is needed to clarify its VDR-specific mechanisms.

      Clinical Implications : Combining these supplements with Vitamin D supplementation may optimize outcomes, especially in deficient populations. Future trials should explore dose-response relationships and synergistic formulations.

      Future Directions

      • Personalized Medicine : Genetic screening for VDR polymorphisms could identify patients most likely to benefit from supplementation 5 14.

      • Combination Therapies : Investigating Resveratrol + Vitamin D or Quercetin + corticosteroids may enhance efficacy while minimizing steroid doses.

      • Long-Term Safety : Despite promising data, prolonged use of high-dose supplements requires rigorous safety assessments.
