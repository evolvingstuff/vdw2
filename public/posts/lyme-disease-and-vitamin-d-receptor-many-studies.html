      Borrelia and the VDR

      In the arms race of host–microbe co-evolution, successful microbial pathogens have evolved ingenious ways to evade host immune responses.9)

      Studies have indicated that the dysregulation of VDR may lead to exaggerated inflammatory responses, raising the possibility that defects in Vitamin D and VDR signaling transduction may be linked to bacterial infection and chronic inflammation. Further characterization of Vitamin D/VDR will help elucidate the pathogenesis of various human diseases and in the design of new approaches for prevention and treatment. Jun Sun 10)

      Since the VDR is at the heart of the innate immune system, bacteria can survive by discovering how to disable it through a variety of different actions. Actions accumulate and are more powerful than individual actions. In keeping with evolutionary theory, a growing number of substances and species have been shown to downregulate the activity of the VDR, one of which is Borrelia burgdorferi. Live Borrelia burgdorferi reduced VDR expression in monocytes (phagocytes) by 50 times, and lysates (“dead” Borrelia) reduced it by 8 times 11)

      Based on the available research, here’s what we know about the potential of those compounds for preventing or treating Lyme disease:

      Resveratrol

      Resveratrol, found in Japanese knotweed, has shown some promise:

      • It has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help against Lyme disease  1 5

      • A 2020 Johns Hopkins study found Japanese knotweed extract had greater activity against Borrelia burgdorferi (the Lyme bacteria) than some antibiotics   5

      • Resveratrol appears safe at typical supplement doses, though it may have blood-thinning effects  5

      Other Compounds

      • Omega-3, magnesium, zinc, quercetin, curcumin:  While these have general anti-inflammatory properties, there’s no strong evidence specifically for Lyme disease prevention or treatment.

      • Berberine:  Some preliminary research suggests potential antimicrobial activity, but more studies are needed on its effects for Lyme disease.

      • Butyrate, sulforaphane, ginger:  No significant evidence was found regarding their use for Lyme disease.

      • Essential oils:  Oregano and thyme oils have shown some in vitro activity against B. burgdorferi, but clinical evidence is lacking   6

      Exercise

      While exercise may have general health benefits, there’s no evidence that “intense exercise” specifically prevents or treats Lyme disease.

      Key Points

      • None of these compounds are proven treatments for Lyme disease.

      • The most effective prevention methods remain avoiding tick bites and prompt tick removal  8

      • Antibiotics are the standard treatment for diagnosed Lyme disease  8

      It’s important to consult with a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment of Lyme disease rather than relying solely on supplements or alternative remedies.
