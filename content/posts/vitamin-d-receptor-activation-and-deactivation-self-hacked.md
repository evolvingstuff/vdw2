#### Natural Ways to Increase Calcitrol and VDR

*  **[Exercise](https://selfhacked.com/blog/top-14-proven-health-benefits-exercise-references-mechanisms/)**  [38](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10954775) – increases calcitriol, but not aerobic exercise [39](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12395212).

*  **[RXR](https://selfhacked.com/blog/importance-real-vitamin-retinol/)**  (and retinol) is needed to produce proteins with the VDR. 1,25D3 binds to the VDR, which then combines with RXR to activate gene expression. (Not all VDR dependent genes need RXR.)

*  **[Parathyroid hormone](https://selfhacked.com/blog/parathyroid-hormone-pth/) (PTH)**  – increases Calcitriol/1,25 D3 [40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6317364) and  **PTH-related peptide**  [41](https://onlinelibrarystatic.wiley.com/store/10.1359/jbmr.070716/asset/5650221105_ftp.pdf;jsessionid=D9E7196C99CE08A42019CBA834AECC8B.f02t01?v=1&t=ijdfx7qj&s=c48e6dc3be36fa31d52ccf17f8316b4e2a98eeb8),

* [ **SIRT1** ](https://selfhacked.com/blog/nad-and-sirt1-their-role-in-chronic-health-issues/) – potentiates VDR [42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25536521), [43](http://press.endocrine.org/doi/abs/10.1210/endo-meetings.2015.BCHVD.6.LBS-042) – acetylation of VDR lessens 1,25D/VDR signaling. SIRT1 increased the ability of VDR to associate with RXR.

*  **[PGC-1a](https://selfhacked.com/blog/about-pgc-1alpha-and-natural-ways-to-increase-it/)** [44](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15908514) – potentiates VDR. It is a coactivator of the VDR, but it still needs 1,25D3.

* [ **Dopamine** ](https://selfhacked.com/blog/dopamine/) [45](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7776973)

*  **[Bile](https://selfhacked.com/blog/bile-supplements/)** – **specifically Lithocholic acid orLCA**  [46](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20371703), The VDR evolved from its ancient role as a detoxification nuclear receptor. LCA is produced from the gut bacteria (metabolizing liver-derived chenodeoxycholic acid). LCA travels to the colon, where the VDR binds to LCA or 1,25 D and activates the [CYP3A4](https://www.selfdecode.com/gene/cyp3a4/?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=selfhacked&utm_campaign=id00002) and SULT2A genes facilitate disposal from the cell via the ABC efflux transporter [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

*  **[Omega-3](https://selfhacked.com/blog/top-22-science-based-health-benefits-of-fish-oil/)** : **DHA, EPA**  [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852), – [Fish oil](https://selfhacked.com/blog/top-22-science-based-health-benefits-of-fish-oil/)/DHA

*  **Omega-6** : **γ-Linolenic acid, [Arachidonic acid](https://selfhacked.com/blog/arachidonic-acid/)**  [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852),

*  **[Curcumin](https://selfhacked.com/blog/curcumin-cures-top-15-scientifically-proven-health-benefits-with-references/)**  [1](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22782502) – Curcumin is more active than LCA/[Bile](https://selfhacked.com/blog/bile-supplements/) in driving VDR-mediated transcription and that it binds to VDR with approximately the same affinity as LCA.

*  **[Resveratrol](https://selfhacked.com/blog/top-15-scientific-health-benefits-of-resveratrol-with-references/)**  [42](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25536521) – Potentiates VDR by: (1) potentiating 1,25D binding to VDR; (2) activating RXR; (3) stimulating [SIRT1](https://selfhacked.com/blog/nad-and-sirt1-their-role-in-chronic-health-issues/)

*  **[Forskolin](https://selfhacked.com/blog/forskolin-everything-know-powerful-substance/)**  [40](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6317364) – increases 1,25D3 from 25D3 in-vitro

*  **Gamma Tocotrienol**  [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852) – Tocotrienols or [Tocopherols](http://www.iherb.com/Now-Foods-Mixed-Tocopherols-Vitamin-E-60-Softgels-Discontinued-Item/52544?rcode=coh780) (IHERB)

*  **Vitamin E** /alpha-tocopherol [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852) – doesn’t compete with calcitriol for the VDR.

*  **Dexamethasone**  [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852) – doesn’t compete with 1,25

* [ **Interferon-gamma** ](https://selfhacked.com/blog/interferon-gamma-how-to-increase-decrease-high-low-levels/) – IFN-γ treatment inhibited 1,25D3 induction of 24-hydroxylase, the enzyme that breaks down 1,25 D3. This means 1,25D3 increased. (Technical: IFNy did not change the base level activity of the promoter, or change 1,25 D binding to the VDR or nuclear VDR levels. IFN-γ impairs VDR-RXR binding to VDRE through a [Stat1](https://www.selfdecode.com/gene/stat1/?utm_source=seo&utm_medium=selfhacked&utm_campaign=id00002)-mediated mechanism) [48](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11909970).

*  **[Estradiol](https://selfhacked.com/blog/estradiol/)** increases VDR expression [49](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9886836), [50](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15930183) and calcitriol levels [51](http://circ.ahajournals.org/content/118/14/1476).

DHA, EPA, linoleic acid and arachidonic acid are all 10,000X less capable than 1,25 D3 at activating the VDR [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

[Curcumin](https://selfhacked.com/blog/curcumin-cures-top-15-scientifically-proven-health-benefits-with-references/) is 1,000X less capable than 1,25 D3 in inducing VDR gene expression [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

Curcumin and bile have a similar binding ability to the VDR and similar levels of gene expression [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

Curcumin, Bile, DHA, EPA, Arachidonic acid all compete with 1,25 D3 for binding. Dexamethasone and alpha-tocopherol don’t compete [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

A natural question to pose would be that if these are competitive binders and have a much lower binding capacity for the VDR, are they of use? The answer seems to be yes.

High concentrations of PUFAs could occur in select cells or tissues and exert bioactivity [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).

Excess Bile/LCA given to rats caused the same effect that 1,25D3 would cause (in particular calcium transport activation) [47](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18844852).Kidney glandular might contain some 1,25 vitamin D.

#### Pathogens That Inhibit The Vitamin D Receptor

Many pathogens inhibit some aspect of the vitamin D system – either the VDR, the ability of molecules to bind to it or the ability of VDR to cause gene expression. These are some examples, but I’m sure I haven’t covered all of them known to the body of science.

*  **P. aeruginosa**  (often hospital acquired). Produces “Sulfonolipid ligand capnine” [61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19758177). Antibiotics don’t work well [62](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudomonas_aeruginosa).

*  **[H. pylori](https://selfhacked.com/blog/h-pylori-part-1/)** (responsible for stomach ulcers). 50% of the global population has this. Produces “Sulfonolipid ligand capnine” [61](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19758177).

*  **Lyme/Borrelia**  – Live Borrelia __reduces VDR by 50 times__ (in [monocytes](https://selfhacked.com/blog/monocytes/)) and “dead” Borrelia reduces it by 8 times [63](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19461888) 

   * This could explain why people develop autoimmune conditions after Lyme infection.

*  **Tuberculosis**  – Reduces VDR 3.3-fold [64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12890386)

*  **“Gliding” [biofilm](http://mpkb.org/home/pathogenesis/microbiota/biofilm) bacteria have been shown to create Capnine**  – Capnine (Cytophaga, Capnocytophaga, Sporocytophaga, and Flexibacter) 

*  **Chlamydia** ( **trachomatis** )

*  **Shigella**  – bacteria in stool and causes intestinal problems and diarrhea. It increases Caspase-3, which is a protein which breaks apart the VDR structure and thus limits the ability of VDR to perform gene transcription [65](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18832097).

* Mycobacterium leprase – produces mir-21 to target multiple genes associated with the VDR [64](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12890386).

*  **Epstein-Barr virus** ( **EBV** ) – Decreases VDR by a factor of about five [66](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19550398) EBV also blocks the ability of VDR to produce products [67](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20593215).

*  **HIV**  – binds to the VDR [68](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9814454) and inhibits conversion to active D [69](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19209727).

*  **Aspergillus fumigatus**  – In cystic fibrosis patients, the fungus A. fumigatus has been shown to secrete gliotoxin, a toxin which dose-dependently decreases VDR.

*  **Cytomegalovirus**  – CMV decreases VDR 2.2 fold [70](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18566437).

*  **Hepatitis C virus**  – Inhibits CYP24A1, the enzyme responsible for breaking down excess 1,25-D [71](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21793032)
