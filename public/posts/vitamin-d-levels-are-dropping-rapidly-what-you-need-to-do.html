      Vitamin D levels are dropping rapidly – what you need to do

      November 12, 2014

      Update: Reasons for Low Vitamin D and what to do

      • About 2X more people are vitamin D deficient now than just two decades ago. military

      • Canada 6% drop in 2 years all ages, about 2X higher % for age 6-11

      • 2X more people have less than :30 ng of vitamin D in a decade – Feb 2010

      • Governments are increasing their Vitamin D recommendations about every decade.

      • Dr. Hollick recommended 1000 IU in 2004, but a decade later recommends 3,500 IU

      • Incidence of over 30 health problems related to vitamin D has doubled in a decade

      • There are a great many reasons for the decreasing vitamin D levels - such as

      • getting less vitamin D from the sun
      • getting less vitamin D from food
      • getting less Magnesium needed to get and use Vitamin D

      10,000 IU of vitamin D is great. I have been having that much for the past 4 summers. (more in winter)

      • Note however, vitamin D monotherapy can cause problems
      • you should also balance your cofactors
      • example: increase Magnesium and Vitamin K2, and decrease Calcium

      Many ways to increase your vitamin D levels

      • Low Cost vitamin D - including liquid and bulk
      • a great many forms of vitamin D, do not have to swallow even a tiny capsule

      • Vitamin D home fortification- don’t wait 100 years for your govt

      • Vitamin D bulb for use in the home - or perhaps office

      • One pill every two weeks gives you all the vitamin D most adults need

      Some people have a high risk of having low vitamin D

      • Obese

      • Seniors

      • Dark Skin

      • Pregnant

      • Shut-in

      • Recent Trauma or surgery
