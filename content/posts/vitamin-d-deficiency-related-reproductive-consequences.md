 **Study prefers 40 ng, but recommends a minimum of 30 ng** 

 **Does not appear to mention several important factors** 

* Get Vitamin D high as soon as possible - before conception is best

   * Can <a href="/posts/loading-dose-of-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Loading Dose of Vitamin D">restore vitamin D levels in less than a week</a>

* Vitamin D helps the [fertility of the man as well as the woman](/categories/fertility-and-sperm)

* <a href="/posts/obese-need-2x-to-3x-more-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Obese need 2X to 3X more vitamin D - Nov 2014">Obese need > 4,000 IU to get to the same blood level</a>

* Various genes can restrict response of Vitamin D in the blood and the tissue 

* <a href="/posts/monthly-120000-iu-vitamin-d-plus-daily-calcium-was-great-during-pregnancies-rct" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Monthly 120,000 IU Vitamin D plus daily Calcium was great during pregnancies – RCT Sept 2017">Monthly Vitamin D may be better than daily</a> (due to genes,  less likely to forget)

* Vitamin D in oil carrier  is not appropriate for ~10% of the population

   * Vitamin D in powder or emulsion provides a better response, about the same price

* <a href="/posts/pregnancy-and-infants-healthier-with-omega-3-supplementation-many-studies" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Pregnancy and infants healthier with Omega-3 supplementation - many studies">Omega-3</a> Magnesium, etc helps preganaices, infants 

   * and gets more Vitamin D into both the blood and tissues
