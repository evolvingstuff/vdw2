The US Institute of Medicine made 4 Vitamin D math mistakes in 2010

The IoM finally agreed that they made math mistakes in 2017

Instead of RDA = 600 IU, it should have been 8,800 IU (14X higher)

However, an IoM panel did not want to be embarrassed by publicly admitting mistakes.

The world copied the US mistake of 600 IU

 **$1 trillion math** 

* [Global health spending for 2018](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240017788) =  $8.3 trillion

* Assume reduce all medical care costs by half

* Assume only half of the people would take lots of vitamin D

* Thus, decrease in health care costs =12%  = half X half X 75%

   * 12% of $8.3 trillion = $1.2 trillion
