Sleep Sci 2023; 16(01): 084-091 DOI: 10.1055/s-0043-1767748

Luiz Ant├┤nio Alves de Menezes-J├║nior , Virg├нnia Capistrano Fajardo , Raimundo Marques do

ObjectiveтАГTo evaluate the association between sleep parameters and hypovitaminosis D in rotating shift drivers.

Material and MethodsтАГWe conducted a cross-sectional study on 82 male rotating shift workers (24тАУ57 years old) with at least one cardiovascular risk factor (such as hyperglycemia, dyslipidemia, abdominal obesity, physical inactivity, hypertension, and smoking). Polysomnography was used to evaluate sleep parameters. Logistic regression was used to model the association between hypovitaminosis D and sleep parameters after adjustment for relevant covariates.

ResultsтАГHypovitaminosis D (< 20тАЙng/mL) was seen in 30.5% of the workers.

Shift workers with hypovitaminosis D had 

* lower sleep efficiency (odds ratio <span>[OR]</span>: 3.68; 95% confidence interval <span>[CI]</span>: 1.95тАУ5.53),

* lower arterial oxygen saturation (OR: 5.35; 95% CI: 3.37тАУ6.12), and 

* increased microarousal index (OR: 3.85; 95% CI: 1.26тАУ5.63) after adjusting.

ConclusionтАГWe suggest that hypovitaminosis D is associated with greater sleep disturbances in rotating shift workers.
