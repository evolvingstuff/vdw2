Today, IвАЩm going to share 22 signs of a magnesium deficiency. Sixty percent of magnesium is in the bone, 20% is in the muscle, 19% is in the soft tissue, and 1% is in the blood.

Magnesium deficiency is the most overlooked deficiency because it cannot be detected by a blood test. 

 **The top  <span style="color:orange;">causes</span> of magnesium deficiency include:** 

вАҐ Consumption of sugar and refined carbs

вАҐ Alcohol consumption 

вАҐ Cirrhosis of the liver

вАҐ NPK fertilizer leads to crops that are low in magnesium

вАҐ Malabsorption from gut inflammation 

вАҐ Diuretics 

вАҐ PPIs 

вАҐ Gastric bypass surgery

вАҐ Low vitamin D 

вАҐ Inflammation

Diabetes, high blood pressure, arthritis, osteoporosis, and cardiovascular disease are all associated with low magnesium. 

The best sources of magnesium are leafy greens, almonds, and dark chocolate, but some of these foods contain oxalates. Oxalates block the absorption of magnesium. Meat, cheese, and shellfish contain magnesium and do not contain oxalates. 

 **Here are 22 surprising <span style="color:orange;">signs</span> of magnesium deficiency:** 

вАҐ Anxiety

вАҐ Insomnia

вАҐ Depression

вАҐ Migraines

вАҐ Restless legs syndrome

вАҐ Calcification of the arteries 

вАҐ Arrhythmias 

вАҐ Stroke

вАҐ Metabolic syndrome

вАҐ Polycystic ovarian syndrome 

вАҐ Kidney stones

вАҐ Higher levels of calcium in the blood

вАҐ Inflammation

вАҐ Nystagmus 

вАҐ Asthma 

 **Neuromuscular excitability, or tetany, is the  <span style="color:orange;">number 1 earliest sign</span> of a magnesium deficiency. Tetany is a twitching of the muscles, often affecting the left eye.** 

* Magnesium is required for proper nerve function. When youвАЩre deficient, the nerves become more excited, leading to faster nerve impulses.
