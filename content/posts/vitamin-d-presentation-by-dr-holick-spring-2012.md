* <a href="/posts/vitamin-d-test" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 266">Vitamin D test</a> is the most ordered blood test in the US {2011?}

* Is your infant vitamin D deficient? Best symptom =  head sweating at night

> Note: there are many other [possible causes for head sweating](http://www.rightdiagnosis.com/symptoms/night_sweats_in_children/causes.htm)

* 750,000 pet reptiles purchased in the US annually – majority do not get vitamin D

> Holick bought a <a href="/posts/40-uvb-bulb" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 982">$40 UVB bulb</a> for his daughter’s pet iguana

* Komono dragons given to US were kept behind windows  were not [fertile](https://www.VitaminDWiki.com/tiki-browse_categories.php?parentId=65&sort_mode=created_desc%20) (no vitamin D)

> After getting UVB they became fertile <a href="/posts/vet-and-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 17">Vet and Vitamin D</a>

* Indians and Pakistani (<a href="/posts/dark-skin" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 1064">dark skin</a>) need 3X-5X as much UVB to make as much vitamin D.  See also: <a href="/posts/middle-east-and-low-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 1758">Middle East and low Vitamin D</a>

* 70% reduction in the ability of <a href="/posts/70-year-olds" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 28">70 year-olds</a> to make vitamin D from the sun

* [Toxicity](/categories/toxicity) not a worry until > 150 nanograms

* <a href="/posts/iom-error" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 1806">IoM error</a> disagreed with the author of the study

> IoM said 20 ng, should have been 30 nanograms

* Even with failed kidney a person can still take vitamin D 

> Vitamin D is also converted in other parts of the body

> Holick typically gives 50,000 IU weekly for 8 weeks as a [loading dose](/categories/loading-dose)

* Every tissue in the body has a vitamin D receptor – must be for some reason

* Higher latitude ==> <a href="/posts/higher-blood-pressure" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 1171">higher blood pressure</a>

* People who work in the sun have LESS melanoma {Perhaps because they do not get sunburn}

* Melanoma occurs on the LEAST exposed portions of the skin

* Should not treat patients with the active form of vitamin D (for example if they have Kidney failure)

> It has a half life of only 4 hours

> It actually lowers the level of vitamin D in the blood ( by making it water soluble)
