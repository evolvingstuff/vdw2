#### Effect of Vitamin D Supplementation on Relapse-Free Survival Among Patients With Digestive Tract Cancers – The AMATERASU Randomized Clinical Trial

JAMA. 2019;321(14):1361-1369. doi:10.1001/jama.2019.2210

Mitsuyoshi Urashima, MD1; Hironori Ohdaira, MD2; Taisuke Akutsu, MD1; et al Shinya Okada, MD3; Masashi Yoshida, MD2; Masaki Kitajima, MD2; Yutaka Suzuki, MD2

<div class="border" style="background-color:#FFFAE2;padding:15px;margin:10px 0;border-radius:5px;width:700px">

A paltry 2,000 IU of vitamin D was given to those diagnosed with digestive tract cancers

Would have had far more benefit if they had done one or more of the following

1. Started with a Vitamin D<a href="/posts/overview-loading-of-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Overview Loading of vitamin D">loading dose</a> (e.g. 400,000 IU over a week)

1. Had used a dose size of at least 4,000 IU of vitamin D

1. Had used <a href="/posts/optimal-vitamin-d-supplementation-strategies-d3-weekly-or-monthly" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Optimal vitamin D supplementation strategies (D3 - weekly or monthly) – Feb 2017">less frequent than daily dosing</a>

1. Had supplemented to increase the [activation of the vitamin D Receptor](/categories/vitamin-d-receptor) - such as Resveratrol
