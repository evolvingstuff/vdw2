 **Summary of 20+ studies** 

1. Loading doses of 300,000 IU Vitamin D have been frequently used for adults

> More than 300,000 IU is sometimes needed for adults

> <a href="/posts/infants-children-might-only-need-10000-iu-per-kg" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This link has an unknown page_id: 7928">infants-Children might only need 10,000 IU per kg</a>

1. Loading dose for the ICU should be given in a single day

> An oral dose does not result in a full response for 3+ days

> An [injection](/categories/injection) does not result in a full response for several weeks 

1. A <a href="/posts/gut-friendly-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Gut-Friendly Vitamin D">gut-friendly</a> form of Vitamin D should be used if patient has poor digestion

> [Nanoemulsion sublingual or topical](/posts/topical-vitamin-d-provides-more-benefits-than-oral-sometimes-many-studies) are fast acting (hours)

> &nbsp; &nbsp;Any form of vitamin D given just before/after surgery is better than none

1.  **All of the above also applies to ICU**  (Intensive Care Unit)

> [4.8 X more likely to die within 28 days of ICU if low Vitamin D - Jan 2024](/posts/48-x-more-likely-to-die-within-28-days-of-icu-if-low-vitamin-d)

1. <a href="/posts/overview-vitamin-d3-not-d2" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Overview Vitamin D3 not D2">Vitamin D3, not Vitamin D2</a> should be used

1. Note: Some <a href="/posts/chemotherapy-might-be-amplified-by-vitamin-d" style="color: red; text-decoration: underline;" title="This post/category does not exist yet: Chemotherapy might be amplified by vitamin D">Chemotherapies are amplified so much by Vitamin D as to</a> result in death if dose size is not decreased
