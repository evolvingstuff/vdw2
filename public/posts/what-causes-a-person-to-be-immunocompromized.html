      What causes a person to be immunocompromized

      September 22, 2024
          • Perplexity AI answer - Sept 2024

      Perplexity AI answer - Sept 2024

      There are several factors that can cause a person to become immunocompromised:

      1. Medical conditions:

      HIV/AIDS

      Cancer, especially cancers of the immune system like leukemia

      Diabetes

      Multiple myeloma

      1. Genetic disorders:

      Primary immunodeficiency disorders like severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID)

      Common variable immunodeficiency (CVID)

      Chronic granulomatous disease (CGD)

      1. Medications and treatments:

      Chemotherapy for cancer

      Immunosuppressive drugs given after organ transplants

      Long-term use of corticosteroids

      Radiation therapy

      1. Other factors:

      Advanced age

      Malnutrition, especially protein deficiency

      Lack of sleep

      Chronic stress

      Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption

      1. Temporary causes:

      Recent infections like influenza or mononucleosis

      Prolonged hospitalization

      1. Removal of immune system organs:

      Spleen removal (splenectomy)

      Being immunocompromised means the immune system is weakened and less able to fight off infections and diseases. This can lead to more frequent, severe, or difficult-to-treat infections. The degree of immunocompromise can vary depending on the underlying cause and may be temporary or long-lasting.
