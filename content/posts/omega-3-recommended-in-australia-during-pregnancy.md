#### [Pregnancy Care Guidelines](https://www.health.gov.au/resources/pregnancy-care-guidelines/part-c-lifestyle-considerations) Austrailan Govt.

*  **"Supplementation with omega-3 long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids (800 mg docosahexaenoic acid <span>[DHA]</span> and 100 mg eicosapentaenoic acid <span>[EPA]</span> per day) may reduce the risk of preterm birth among women who are low in omega-3."** 

*  **"Iodine requirements increase during pregnancy and a supplement of 150 micrograms a day is advised."** 

*  **"Vitamin D: Vitamin D supplementation may be a consideration for women with vitamin D levels lower than 50 nmol/L. The evidence for vitamin D supplementation is discussed in Chapter 47. "**
