                                          Asked Bard about High intensity exercise increasing the vitamin D getting to cells - Dec 6, 2023

                                          The evidence suggests that high intensity exercise may increase the amount of vitamin D getting to cells, though the effects are complex and depend on several factors. Here’s a breakdown of what we know:

                                          Potential mechanisms for increased vitamin D delivery:

                                          • Mobilization from storage: Exercise can stimulate the release of vitamin D from adipose tissue and other storage sites, making it available for circulation and uptake by cells.

                                          • Increased blood flow: High-intensity exercise increases blood flow, which can facilitate the delivery of vitamin D to various tissues.

                                          • Enhanced VDR expression: Vitamin D receptors (VDRs) are essential for cellular uptake and utilization of vitamin D. Studies suggest that exercise may increase the expression of VDRs in certain tissues, leading to improved vitamin D utilization.

                                          • Hormonal changes: Exercise induces the release of hormones like catecholamines and growth hormone, which might influence vitamin D metabolism and transport.

                                          _Supporting evidence:

                                          Studies have shown that high-intensity exercise can significantly increase serum 25-hydroxyvitamin D (25(OH)D) levels in vitamin D-deficient individuals. This suggests improved mobilization and circulation of vitamin D.

                                          Research indicates that a single bout of high-intensity interval training can lead to a temporary increase in circulating 25(OH)D levels.

                                          Some studies suggest that regular high-intensity exercise may be more effective than moderate-intensity exercise in increasing vitamin D status.

                                          However, there are also some caveats to consider:

                                          • The effect of exercise on vitamin D levels might depend on factors like baseline vitamin D status, training volume, and individual differences in response to exercise.

                                          • More research is needed to fully understand the long-term impact of high-intensity exercise on vitamin D metabolism and cellular uptake.

                                          Overall, while high-intensity exercise may offer some benefits for vitamin D delivery to cells, it’s important to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice regarding vitamin D supplementation and exercise strategies. They can assess your individual needs and recommend the best course of action based on your specific situation.

                                          Research Supporting the Relationship Between High Intensity Exercise and Vitamin D Levels:

                                          “Impact of Vitamin D on Physical Efficiency and Exercise Performance—A Review” (2020): This review discusses the connection between vitamin D and physical performance, highlighting its role in muscle function and immune response. It also mentions that exercise, specifically endurance training, can increase vitamin D levels in individuals with deficiency.

                                          “Exercise: A Possibly Effective Way to Improve Vitamin D Nutritional Status” (2022): This article explores the potential of exercise to improve vitamin D status, particularly in individuals with deficiency. It suggests that endurance exercise can promote the release of vitamin D metabolites from adipose tissue , ultimately increasing serum 25(OH)D levels.

                                          “High-Intensity Physical Activity with High Serum Vitamin D Levels is Associated with a Low Prevalence of Osteopenia and Osteoporosis: A Population-Based Study” (2021): This study investigated the relationship between high-intensity physical activity, vitamin D levels, and bone health. It found a link between high-intensity activity and lower prevalence of osteopenia and osteoporosis, especially when combined with sufficient vitamin D levels.
