 **Caution on mega-doses doesn’t alter the facts or urgency of fixing low D** 

June 5, 2020 (updated July 8)        [Karl Pfleger](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.linkedin.com/in/karl-pfleger-955085159/&sa=D&ust=1595848790245000&usg=AOvVaw0uSu3AK2mivxGYMBmDNrnc), <span>[mailto:karl@agingbiotech.info]</span> (I declare no conflicts.)

 **There’s no reason not to fix low vitamin D & much evidence it could save many lives. Until the data becomes inconsistent with that possibility, fixing low D while getting more data must be prioritized.** 

#### Facts

1. It’s widely agreed that low vitamin D (<30ng/ml) results in worse health & more deaths. [1]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0170791&sa=D&ust=1595848790260000&usg=AOvVaw2e9MVd0IYRluYoNd6TkR4w"),[2]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.bmj.com/content/348/bmj.g1903&sa=D&ust=1595848790262000&usg=AOvVaw33xr-bv9wU8Cn-UxaLkz-a"),[3]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0960076017300316&sa=D&ust=1595848790264000&usg=AOvVaw3z3KrL9ZQ50Q-OWRrwrr3b"),[4]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.grassrootshealth.net/project/our-scientists/&sa=D&ust=1595848790266000&usg=AOvVaw1bWWwGo71WGIaLX5eCoXLC")

1. Causal evidence shows [D protects against respiratory infections (40 RCTs, n=30k)](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.07.14.20152728v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790268000&usg=AOvVaw2aDLNgQgGZC7m2GaOcjtPX) & [lung injury](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.spandidos-publications.com/10.3892/mmr.2017.7546&sa=D&ust=1595848790270000&usg=AOvVaw3FeoysgnEeN0H0ldwtMeGv).

1. Low D is [prevalent](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532266/&sa=D&ust=1595848790272000&usg=AOvVaw3UU3hBSMuE_xfwwqKCQ5v6) [worldwide](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4018438/&sa=D&ust=1595848790274000&usg=AOvVaw1V56oyus3Bu9ZJKu7IcJlt). USA: [40% D<20ng/ml](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6075634/&sa=D&ust=1595848790276000&usg=AOvVaw1oPlvXQQQTHF0NQdiqbYBH), [64% healthy D<30](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3755751/&sa=D&ust=1595848790277000&usg=AOvVaw05BoyCgdJmcfc8TBgHd0km). [96% nursing home D<30](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12820811/&sa=D&ust=1595848790279000&usg=AOvVaw3Lo_l2kLYpO8hHU92xbU0j).

1. D’s RDA was [set 10x too low](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28768407/&sa=D&ust=1595848790282000&usg=AOvVaw2nQdPKHU5uqmCBE5P3UJlY) due to [a mistake](https://www.google.com/url?q=http://carolabinder.blogspot.com/2018/03/d-is-for-devastating-statistical-error.html&sa=D&ust=1595848790284000&usg=AOvVaw3OLzOluJL6N1ar6yPHpid7). 30ng/ml [needs 6200 IU/day](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4377874/&sa=D&ust=1595848790286000&usg=AOvVaw0GATaS8PcaSNBh_J0LCL_W). [Experts say raise RDA](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/B9780128099650000598&sa=D&ust=1595848790288000&usg=AOvVaw1CtCQV8nZR8y2jVNeQ8tx4).

1. D is safe: [Toxicity unlikely <80ng/ml](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5045493/&sa=D&ust=1595848790292000&usg=AOvVaw2A2pzlXQCJo3SIpIb-ykQ3). [19 orgs say 4000 IU/day safe](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://VitaminDWiki.com/4%2C000+IU+of+Vitamin+D+is+OK+-+19+organizations+agree+-+2018&sa=D&ust=1595848790294000&usg=AOvVaw2SO8hJ3BVi3LyjrC3YAUZv), [Endocrine Society says 10,000](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/B9780128099650000598&sa=D&ust=1595848790296000&usg=AOvVaw1uKYj08SbiCBjDwHJqLLAW).

1. Many causal biological mechanisms argue D especially important against COVID-19. [1]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/12/4/988/htm&sa=D&ust=1595848790298000&usg=AOvVaw0pFesPjGsqhbI2YYUGa3Su"),[2]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202004.0355/v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790300000&usg=AOvVaw0igrRRvao7ZOzAdWc0qS7o"),[3]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202005.0265/v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790302000&usg=AOvVaw1WOa7r8qWQ7KQt6CX9SIvP"),[4]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://covid19-evidence.paho.org/handle/20.500.12663/1569&sa=D&ust=1595848790304000&usg=AOvVaw3lJz7JaoD3u4AzTqwn9m7a"),[5]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32455629/&sa=D&ust=1595848790306000&usg=AOvVaw23BurIcrrxBFQCuHE6LupD"),[6]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7246956/&sa=D&ust=1595848790307000&usg=AOvVaw0ZkUaXZk-MCT6z8cV-KVpl"),[7]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Suresh_Sharma42/publication/341752269_Vitamin_D_A_cheap_yet_effective_bullet_against_coronavirus_disease-19_-_Are_we_convinced_yet/links/5eda7b2692851c9c5e81e390/Vitamin-D-A-cheap-yet-effective-bullet-against-coronavirus-disease-19-Are-we-convinced-yet.pdf&sa=D&ust=1595848790312000&usg=AOvVaw1rqktgxwtTaQHqb1EmM1LU")

1. Calcitriol (active form vitD) has direct inhibitory effect on SARS-CoV-2 in human epithelial cells. [1]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.06.21.162396v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790316000&usg=AOvVaw0zkfBiYZC7630qCxATxvsj")

1. Severe COVID-19 correlates strongly (eg, 3x likelier) w/ low D across many studies. [1]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.07.01.20144329v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790318000&usg=AOvVaw1W1MLxPoUahnEbvHo_SKTO"),[2]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.06.21.20136903v2&sa=D&ust=1595848790320000&usg=AOvVaw3BVorSb920eDiqXN47lhlh"),[3]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.05.01.20079376v2&sa=D&ust=1595848790322000&usg=AOvVaw2BOMmpTrb87LAcscdCUeMN"),[4]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.04.24.20075838v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790323000&usg=AOvVaw3LXedYYJOYXVhltKA6CIDL"),[5]("https://www.google.com/url?q=http://imj.ie/vitamin-d-deficiency-and-ards-after-sars-cov-2-infection/&sa=D&ust=1595848790325000&usg=AOvVaw0_5uCQQ2CPSxqN4St3MK5w"),[6]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://europepmc.org/article/ppr/ppr166270&sa=D&ust=1595848790326000&usg=AOvVaw0Xyvp4-_xpuq57a9iXgqkn"),[7]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://europepmc.org/article/ppr/ppr165763&sa=D&ust=1595848790328000&usg=AOvVaw2f1ucUYt-3c_8vsBreT_3E")

1. [Causal Inference Modeling](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.05.01.20087965v2&sa=D&ust=1595848790330000&usg=AOvVaw2AKUpb_flDF6Eya8cDoxEA) & [Mendelian Randomization](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.05.01.20079376v2&sa=D&ust=1595848790332000&usg=AOvVaw0FUe0EEes4e1fjbite6-RK) show D’s effect on COVID-19 to be causal.

1. 1st controlled trial: [1000 IU/d D +Mg&B12 group needed oxygen 6.5x less](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.06.01.20112334v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790335000&usg=AOvVaw1F-FZlzZz0MxTQeVy0vyrj), adj. f/ age, sex, comorb.

#### Imperatives

1. Hospitals & doctors should test the vitamin D blood level of all COVID-19 positive patients, and medical records holders should release analyses or datasets of D level vs case severity.

1. COVID-19 standard-of-care should change to include testing & raising D levels (to >=30ng/ml), and care facilities should report differences in outcomes vs before doing so (as in fact #10 study).

1. Governments should prioritize eradicating insufficient D (<30ng/ml) as a top priority for controlling the pandemic, alongside distancing & masks. This requires clear, widespread messaging & testing.

1. The [NAM](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Academy_of_Medicine&sa=D&ust=1595848790342000&usg=AOvVaw27O-MYBJD4642KNk4iw9tt), FDA, & analogs should increase recs (RDA/DV/etc) to 4000IU (0.1mg) until end of crisis.

1. The FDA should require all COVID19 clinical trials (new & underway) to test D levels of all subjects. New trials should also treat D insufficiency in both arms to find efficacy in more than the D deprived.

#### Objections Dismissed

1. Correlation isn’t causation: Facts 2,6,7,9,&10 are causal evidence very consistent w/ the other data.

1. Experts urge caution / RCTs needed: Caution warnings apply only to mega-doses, for which RCTs would be needed. No one suggests anyone keep D levels too low. RCTs to fix low D are unethical.

1. Preprints might be flawed: True, but when so much evidence is consistent, removing a few pieces wouldn’t change much. Authors of facts 7-10 preprints were vetted. 3 questionable preprints were omitted. Facts 1-5 plus any few of the 15+ new papers would be convincing enough. The biggest study from fact 8 is [fully peer reviewed and accepted for publication](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/febs.15495&sa=D&ust=1595848790349000&usg=AOvVaw16nrVmlf--oY0PU9-J1UWI).

1. Some D supplement trials show no benefit: Most such trials have subjects with already sufficient D, use monthly mega-doses, use D2, or use doses too low to achieve sufficiency (see fact 4).

1. Studies show D level not related to C19 infection rate [1]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32413819/&sa=D&ust=1595848790354000&usg=AOvVaw0yZS2_lUXEZsrh1KCNvVyN"),[2]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.04.29.20084277v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790355000&usg=AOvVaw3cCuAQXh9FVQM5EVjGUSpV"): These studies used 10+ yr-old D tests & are contradicted by better studies w/ recent tests [3]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/febs.15495&sa=D&ust=1595848790357000&usg=AOvVaw3tIKxN7YUBxUscnsGJ8zgu"),[4]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/12/5/1359/htm&sa=D&ust=1595848790358000&usg=AOvVaw1aOJyS2FLIPXPvuPOa9Zi7"),[5]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.05.08.20095893v1&sa=D&ust=1595848790359000&usg=AOvVaw2FOoFHyrEVKY9JHQC-EjTB"), w/ [3]("https://www.google.com/url?q=https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/febs.15495&sa=D&ust=1595848790360000&usg=AOvVaw30pyYoNJiOvtQJqkDZ7_oE") biggest & peer reviewed. Also, trials showing no fewer infections do not cast doubt on the evidence for disease severity (facts 8-10).
