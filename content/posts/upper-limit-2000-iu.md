Long-term intakes above the UL increase the risk of adverse health effects [<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en4">4</a>](<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en4">4</a>)  (Table 4). Substantially larger doses administered for a short time or  periodically (e.g., 50,000 IU/week for 8 weeks) do not cause toxicity.  Rather, the excess is stored and used as needed to maintain normal serum  25(OH)D concentrations when vitamin D intakes or sun exposure are  limited [<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en11">11</a>,<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en92">92</a>](<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en11">11</a>,<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en92">92</a>).<br />

Several nutrition scientists recently challenged these ULs, first published in 1997 [<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en92">92</a>](<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en92">92</a>).  They point to newer clinical trials conducted in healthy adults and   conclude that the data support a UL as high as 10,000 IU/day. Although  vitamin D supplements above recommended levels given in clinical trials  have not shown harm, most trials were not adequately designed to assess  harm [<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en5">5</a>](<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en5">5</a>).  Evidence is not sufficient to determine the potential risks of excess  vitamin D in infants, children, and women of reproductive age.<br />

<br />

As  noted earlier, the FNB is currently reviewing data to determine whether  updates to the DRIs (including the ULs) for vitamin D are appropriate [<a class="fscopy_nounderline" href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en4">4</a>](<a%20class="fscopy_nounderline"%20href="http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/vitamind.asp#en4">4</a>).<br />
