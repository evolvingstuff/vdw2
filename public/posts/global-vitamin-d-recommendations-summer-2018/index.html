      4,000 IU

      • Seniors need at least 4,000 IU vitamin D, no test needed – Consensus Jan 2014

      • 50,000 IU of vitamin D every two weeks – Jordan conclusion - RCT July 2017

      • One pill every two weeks gives you all the vitamin D most adults need

        • 50,000 every 2 weeks = 3,600 IU (which is less than the 4,000-10,000 IU upper limits)

      • Vitamin D - at least 4,000 IU to achieve 40-60 ng and reduce risk of early death – Holick June 2018

        • Note - 6,000 IU needed for many to achieve 40 ng of Vitamin D - Feb 2017

      30 ng

      • US admits math mistake was made in 2010 in estimating Vitamin D, but will not change recommendations – Nov 2017

        • Based on old IoM data: 3,000 IU is needed to get 97.5% of the population to 30 ng

        • Most groups consider 30 ng to be the minimim adequate amount

        • hundreds of clinical trials since the IoM report have found that 3,000 IU is not enough

      40 ng

      • Optimal level of vitamin D is 40 ng, 93 percent of Canadians do not have optimal levels – Oct 2017

      • Vitamin D more than 40 ng: 1300 IU 50% chance: 5,000 IU 80% chance - Aug 2014

      • Vitamin D recommendations by Italian Endocrinologists – 40 ng if pregnant, 30 ng if high risk – April 2018

      50 ng

      • Is 50 ng of vitamin D too high, just right, or not enough

      • Vitamin D is needed for human fertility – goal is 50 ng – Sept 2018
