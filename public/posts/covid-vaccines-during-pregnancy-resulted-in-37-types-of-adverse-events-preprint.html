      Later Published: COVID vaccines during pregnancy cause 37 types of problems, CDC still recommends them – Feb 2025

      Are COVID-19 Vaccines in Pregnancy as Safe and Effective as the U.S. Government, Medical Organizations, and Pharmaceutical Industry Claim? Part I

      Preprints 2024, 2024062062. https://doi.org/10.20944/preprints202406.2062.v1

      James Thorp * , Albert Benavides , Maggie Thorp , Daniel McDyer , Kimberly Biss , Julie Threet , Peter McCullough

      Version 1 : Received: 27 June 2024 / Approved: 28 June 2024 / Online: 29 June 2024 (00:12:19 CEST)

      Introduction: In Part I of this three-part series we report a retrospective, population-based cohort study assessing rates of adverse events (AEs) in pregnancy after COVID-19 vaccines compared to the same AEs after influenza vaccines and after all other vaccines. Methods: Data were collected from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and prevention (CDC) and the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The CDC/FDA Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) database was queried from January 1, 1990, to April 26, 2024 , for adverse events (AEs) involving pregnancy complications following COVID-19 vaccination. The time-period included 412 months for all vaccines except COVID-19 vaccines, having been used for only 40 of the 412 months (December 1, 2020, to April 26, 2024). Proportional reporting ratios (PRR) by time compared AEs after COVID-19 vaccination to those after influenza vaccination, and after all other vaccine products administered to pregnant women. In cases in which the PRR was not applicable, Chi-square analysis and Fisher’s exact tests were used according to CDC/FDA guidance. CDC/FDA stipulate a safety concern if a PRR is ≥ 2 or if a Chi-square is ≥ 4.

      Results: The CDC/FDA’s safety signals were breached for all 37 AEs following COVID-19 vaccination in pregnancy: miscarriage

      1. fetal chromosomal abnormality

      2. fetal malformation

      3. cervical insufficiency

      4. premature rupture of membranes

      5. premature labor

      6. premature delivery

      7. placental calcification

      8. placental infarction

      9. placental thrombosis

      10. placenta accreta

      11. placental abruption

      12. placental insufficiency

      13. placental disorder

      14. fetal maternal hemorrhage

      15. fetal growth restriction

      16. reduced amniotic fluid volume

      17. preeclampsia

      18. fetal heart rate abnormality

      19. fetal cardiac disorder

      20. fetal vascular mal-perfusion

      21. fetal arrhythmia

      22. fetal distress

      23. fetal biophysical profile abnormal

      24. hemorrhage in pregnancy

      25. fetal cardiac arrest

      26. fetal death (stillbirth)

      27. premature infant death

      28. neonatal asphyxia

      29. neonatal dyspnea

      30. neonatal infection

      31. neonatal hemorrhage

      32. insufficient breast milk

      33. neonatal pneumonia

      34. neonatal respiratory distress

      35. neonatal respiratory distress syndrome

      36. and neonatal seizure.

      All p values were ≤ 0.001 with the majority being <0.000001.

      Summary statistics for the deviation from the CDC/FDA safety signals mean (n range) are as follows:

      • PRR 69.3 (46 5.37 - 499); z statistic 9.64 (46 3.29 - 27.0); and

      • Chi-square was 74.7 (26 28.9 - 148).

      Note: Billions of influenza vaccinations have been given during the 400 month coverage of VAERS

      Conclusions: We found unacceptably high breaches in safety signals for 37 AEs after COVID-19 vaccination in pregnant women. An immediate global moratorium on COVID-19 vaccination during pregnancy is warranted. The United States government. medical organizations, hospitals, and pharmaceutical companies have misled and/or deceived the public regarding the safety of COVID-19 vaccination in pregnancy. Promotion of these products must be immediately halted.
