      FAQ – Pregnancy

      March 15, 2016

      Can vitamin D help me get pregnant?

      Yes – vitamin D improves the fertility of both the man and the woman, in both Vitamin D also helps with PCOS

      Are there side effects, how can I avoid them?

      The side effects of vitamin D are extremely rare, and mild, even during pregnancy

      I cannot afford the $20 for vitamin D during my pregnancy – what are my options?

      One bottle of Vitamin D can be shared among several women. A $20 bottle holds 100 capsules of 50,000 IU of vitamin D. At a low level of dosing (<4,000 IU average daily) one bottle can be used for 4 pregnancies, such that the average cost per pregnancy is just $5.

      I am in my late 30s – will vitamin D help me? (advanced maternal age > 35)

      The benefits of vitamin D during pregnancy do not decrease with age, and there are reasons to expect that the benefits may even increase with age

      Just started 3rd trimester, is it too late for vitamin D to help?

      A good fraction of the benefits of vitamin D are still available in the third trimester. In fact most of the pregnancy studies use daily dosing, so the vitamin D levels are not restored until the third trimester anyway. To get the vitamin D benefit when starting in third trimester It is very important to start with a loading dose. A loading dose get the vitamin D levels restored in a week instead of 2-3 months.
