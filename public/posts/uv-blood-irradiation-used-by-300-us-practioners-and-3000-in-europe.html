      What are some of the studies of red or infrared light used to treat health problems - Asked Perplexity AI June 2024

      Several studies have investigated the use of red and infrared light therapy for treating various health conditions:

      1. Wound healing: NASA-funded research demonstrated that high-intensity red and near-infrared LEDs significantly accelerated the healing of oxygen-deprived wounds in rats and sped up the growth of skin, bone, and muscle cell cultures.

      2. Musculoskeletal injuries: Studies with U.S. Navy crews showed that LED devices produced over 40% greater improvement in musculoskeletal injuries and 50% faster healing time for lacerations compared to control groups.

      3. Eye conditions: Research suggested that irradiation with red LED arrays could prevent methanol-induced blindness in rats, leading to proposals for using light therapy to treat retinal ailments like glaucoma and age-related macular degeneration.

      4. Pain and inflammation: The WARP 10 device, developed based on NASA research, was FDA-cleared for temporary relief of minor muscle and joint pain, arthritis, and muscle spasms.

      5. Oral mucositis: A clinical trial using the WARP 75 device successfully treated acute sores in the mucus membranes of patients undergoing radiation and chemotherapy for bone marrow transplants.

      6. Skin conditions: Studies have shown promising results for using red light therapy to treat acne vulgaris, both alone and in combination with blue light therapy.

      7. Hair growth: A study published in the Journal of Cosmetic and Laser Therapy found that red light therapy increased hair density in men with androgenetic alopecia.

      8. Rheumatoid arthritis: Research published in the Journal of Clinical Rheumatology indicated that red light therapy helped reduce pain and inflammation in patients with rheumatoid arthritis.

      9. Aging and wrinkles: Some studies suggest that red light therapy may help reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles by stimulating collagen production and improving circulation.

      While these studies show promising results, it’s important to note that more extensive clinical trials are still needed to fully understand the efficacy of red light therapy for many of these conditions.
