 **This paper does not appear to address the following issues** 

:: **probably because there is not enough data to estimate of "Level of Evidence"** ::

* The amount of Magnesium needed appears to be proportional to body weight

* Some forms of Magnesium are much less likely to cause bowel problems than others

* Taking Magnesium several times per day allows more Mg to be taken daily without bowel problems

> [Animals get more Magnesium than human RDA, and not as a single serving](/posts/animals-get-more-magnesium-than-human-rda-and-not-as-a-single-serving)

* Cellular Mg tests are more reliable and easier to understand and more expensive than serum test

> [Low serum Magnesium readings in ICU were wrong 80 percent of the time – July 2017](/posts/low-serum-magnesium-readings-in-icu-were-wrong-80-percent-of-the-time)

* DMSO greatly increases topical Magnesium effectiveness

* Some claim that [High Fructose Corn Syrup consumes 2X more Magnesium](/posts/fructose-high-fructose-corn-syrup-consumes-2x-more-magnesium-than-sugar)

* Should not try to get your food Magnesium from a single supplier - example, avoid having a single supplier of almonds
