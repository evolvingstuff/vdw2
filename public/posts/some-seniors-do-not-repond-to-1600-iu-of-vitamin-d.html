      Some seniors do not repond to 1600 IU of vitamin D

      April 22, 2012

      Half of seniors did not respond to 1600 IU of vitamin D daily for a year

      RED square = reduction in serum level

      {IMG(fileId=“2125”)}{IMG}

      from slide presentation: https://www.VitaminDWiki.com/tiki-index.php?page_id=2269

      Possible reasons for non-response include, but are not limited to, the following

      • Lacking co-factors Magnesium, etc.

      • Surgery or other trauma during the year decreased vitamin D levels

      Taking drugs by seniors was associated with about 2 ng less vitamin D – March 2012

      • Taking drugs such as Statins, Antiseizure, Prednisone, Orlistat, Questran, Dilatin, Phenobarbital, and Rifampin,

      • Non-alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (4 in 10 seniors) and Vitamin D

      • People with gut problems are low on vitamin D

      • Poor kidney function or poor endocrine function – to make the active form of vitamin D

      See also VitaminDWiki

      • 1600 IU vitamin D was enough to get all (white, non-obese) elderly to 20 ng – RCT Dec 2012
