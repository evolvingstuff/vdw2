      Global Burden of Guillain-Barré Syndrome Explodes in Modern Era of Excessive Vaccination

      substack

      “Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS) was previously considered a rare disease that occurs when the body’s immune system attacks the peripheral nervous system. This damage causes muscle weakness, tingling, numbness, and sometimes paralysis and loss of control over the bowel and bladder.”

      “Jeung et al analyzed the WHO Vigibase vaccine safety system with >8 million reports and focused on 15,377 cases of GBS as summarized by TrialSite News. The average case had GBS **develop 5.5 days after a vaccine shot.”

      From: Global burden of vaccine-associated Guillain-Barré syndrome over 170 countries from 1967 to 2023 - oct 2024 DOI: 10.1038/s41598-024-74729-2
