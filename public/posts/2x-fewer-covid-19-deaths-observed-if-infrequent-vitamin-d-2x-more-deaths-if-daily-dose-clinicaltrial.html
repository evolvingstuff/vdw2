      2X fewer COVID-19 deaths observed if infrequent vitamin D (2X more deaths if daily dose) – ClinicalTrial

      November 9, 2020

      VITAMIN D TREATMENT IS ASSOCIATED WITH REDUCED RISK OF MORTALITY IN PATIENTS WITH COVID-19: A CROSS-SECTIONAL MULTI-CENTRE OBSERVATIONAL STUDY

      Stephanie F Ling MBChB,2, Eleanor Broad MBChB1, Rebecca Murphy MBChB1, Joseph M Pappachan MD 2,3,4, Satveer Pardesi-Newton BM5, Marie-France Kong DM6, Edward B Jude MD1，2,4.

      986 patients, 3 centers in the UK Clinical Trial

      50% had <16 ng of Vitamin D

      75% had <20 ng of Vitamin D

      3 Vitamin D treatments

      • None = control group (>20 ng?)

      • Maintenance therapy (800-2,000IU daily) - 2X INCREASE in deaths

      • High-dose booster therapy (up to 300,000IU total, split over multiple doses) PDF
        2X DECREASE in deaths

        • a loading regimen to provide a total of approximately 300,000 units vitamin D, given either as separate weekly or daily doses over 6 to 10 weeks :

        • The exact regimen will depend on local preference and local availability of vitamin D preparations but could include: 20,000 units capsules, two given weekly for 7 weeks (280,000 units)

      Suspect why this happened (in the UK)

      The UK considers < 12 ng = deficient, and so a loading/restoring dose of 300,000 IU is given

      A person with 13 ng to xx ng considered not to be deficient, so is given a maintenance dose

          which keeps them low, and probably vulnerable to COVID-19 infection

      Note: This Clinical Trial had no randomization, Dosing was based on pre-existing Vitamin D levels
