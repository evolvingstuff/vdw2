    High Omega-3 reduced risk of Ischemic stroke by 18%

    January 5, 2024

    Omega-3 Blood Levels and Stroke Risk: A Pooled and Harmonized Analysis of 183вҖү291 Participants From 29 Prospective Studies

    Stroke. 2024;55:50вҖ“58 https://doi.org/10.1161/STROKEAHA.123.044281

    James H. OвҖҷKeefe, Nathan L. Tintle, William S. Harris, Evan L. OвҖҷKeefe, Aleix Sala-Vila, John Attia, G. Manohar Garg, Alexis Hure, вҖҰ

    BACKGROUND:

    The effect of marine omega-3 PUFAs on risk of stroke remains unclear.

    METHODS:

    We investigated the associations between circulating and tissue omega-3 PUFA levels and incident stroke (total, ischemic, and hemorrhagic) in 29 international prospective cohorts. Each site conducted a de novo individual-level analysis using a prespecified analytical protocol with defined exposures, covariates, analytical methods, and outcomes; the harmonized data from the studies were then centrally pooled. Multivariable-adjusted HRs and 95% CIs across omega-3 PUFA quintiles were computed for each stroke outcome.

    RESULTS:

    Among 183вҖ…291 study participants, there were 10вҖ…561 total strokes, 8220 ischemic strokes, and 1142 hemorrhagic strokes recorded over a median of 14.3 years follow-up. For eicosapentaenoic acid, comparing quintile 5 (Q5, highest) with quintile 1 (Q1, lowest), total stroke incidence was 17% lower (HR, 0.83 [CI, 0.76вҖ“0.91]; P<0.0001), and ischemic stroke was 18% lower (HR, 0.82 [CI, 0.74вҖ“0.91]; P<0.0001). For docosahexaenoic acid, comparing Q5 with Q1, there was a 12% lower incidence of total stroke (HR, 0.88 [CI, 0.81вҖ“0.96]; P=0.0001) and a 14% lower incidence of ischemic stroke (HR, 0.86 [CI, 0.78вҖ“0.95]; P=0.0001). Neither eicosapentaenoic acid nor docosahexaenoic acid was associated with a risk for hemorrhagic stroke. These associations were not modified by either baseline history of AF or prevalent CVD.

    CONCLUSIONS:

    Higher omega-3 PUFA levels are associated with lower risks of total and ischemic stroke but have no association with hemorrhagic stroke.

BACKGROUND:
The effect of marine omega-3 PUFAs on risk of stroke remains unclear.

METHODS:
We investigated the associations between circulating and tissue omega-3 PUFA levels and incident stroke (total, ischemic, and hemorrhagic) in 29 international prospective cohorts. Each site conducted a de novo individual-level analysis using a prespecified analytical protocol with defined exposures, covariates, analytical methods, and outcomes; the harmonized data from the studies were then centrally pooled. Multivariable-adjusted HRs and 95% CIs across omega-3 PUFA quintiles were computed for each stroke outcome.

RESULTS:
Among 183вҖ…291 study participants, there were 10вҖ…561 total strokes, 8220 ischemic strokes, and 1142 hemorrhagic strokes recorded over a median of 14.3 years follow-up. For eicosapentaenoic acid, comparing quintile 5 (Q5, highest) with quintile 1 (Q1, lowest), total stroke incidence was 17% lower (HR, 0.83 [CI, 0.76вҖ“0.91]; P<0.0001), and ischemic stroke was 18% lower (HR, 0.82 [CI, 0.74вҖ“0.91]; P<0.0001). Neither eicosapentaenoic acid nor docosahexaenoic acid was associated with a risk for hemorrhagic stroke. These associations were not modified by either baseline history of AF or prevalent CVD.

CONCLUSIONS:
Higher omega-3 PUFA levels are associated with lower risks of total and ischemic stroke but have no association with hemorrhagic stroke.
