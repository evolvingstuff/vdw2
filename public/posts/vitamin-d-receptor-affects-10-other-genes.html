      6. Immune system genes: VDR affects various genes involved in immune function, including cytokines and their receptors.

      8. Detoxification genes: VDR regulates genes involved in xenobiotic metabolism, such as CYP3A4 .

#Detoxification genes: VDR regulates genes involved in xenobiotic metabolism, such as__ ((Changes in CYP3A4 gene affects Vitamin D, health, reactions to some drugs, etc - June 2022|CYP3A4))__.
#Epigenetic modifiers: VDR can influence the expression of genes involved in __((Epigenetics and Vitamin D – many studies|epigenetic)) regulation__, potentially affecting a wide range of cellular processes.
#Tumor suppressor genes: In some contexts, VDR can regulate genes involved in __cancer prevention and progression.__
It's important to note that the specific genes affected by VDR can vary depending on cell type, tissue context, and physiological conditions. 
The VDR is estimated to regulate about __3-5% of the human genome__, potentially influencing thousands of genes directly or indirectly. 
The complex interplay between VDR, its target genes, and various environmental and genetic factors contributes to the wide-ranging effects of vitamin D in the body
